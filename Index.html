Sarvnipun Kaur

Ph.D. Candidate, Economics, Plaksha University

I am Ph.D candidate pursuing her research in Economics. I have completed my MPhil in Economics from the Department of Economics, Panjab University, and my work experience includes working as a guest faculty in the Department and as a consultant across different policy consulting firms.

As a consultant, I have drafted several white papers, policy recommendations, and other documents (including 2 chapters in the Economic Survey of Punjab- 2019-20). I have experience in liaising and engaging with different government department functionaries and developing concept notes and project proposals for state government reforms. My latest stint included working as a Senior Research Analyst at a boutique regulatory research and policy consultancy called Trayas. I co-authored the State of Discrimination Report and designed its methodology at Trayas. The report was recently tweeted about by the Prime minister’s Economic Advisory Council, and written about in The Week magazine by Dr. Bibek Debroy.

I enjoy teaching because it helps me learn better. My teaching experience includes teaching introductory microeconomics, macroeconomics, and Indian economy to students of Masters and Bachelors programs at Panjab University and Chandigarh University. I have the capability of understanding complex theoretical concepts and breaking them down into simplified and understandable components. I assisted Professor Kriti with her course on Fundamentals of Microeconomics (FM124) at Plaksha University, where I learnt how to o teach mathematical concepts and methods with the same level of precision and rigour.

My research interests revolve around interstate comparisons, regulatory research, and factor markets in India. During the course of my research, I have learnt to perform thorough surveys of literature and develop constructs to measure abstract concepts like discrimination/flexibility. I am trying to supplement my knowledge of qualitative research methods with quantitative tools and statistical programming languages.
